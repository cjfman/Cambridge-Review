## 1. Cambridge STV Algorithm (City Council Elections)

Cambridge uses Single Transferable Vote (STV) proportional representation for its 9-seat City Council
and 6-seat School Committee elections. The specific variant is sometimes called "Cambridge STV."

Legal authority: Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 54A
Counting software: ChoicePlus Pro v2.3.4 (Voting Solutions) with "Cambridge Rules" configuration

### 1a. Quota (Droop)

  quota = floor(total_valid_ballots / (seats + 1)) + 1

Example (2025 council): 23,719 ballots / 10 = 2,371.9 → quota = 2,372

### 1b. Round Structure

Each round proceeds as follows:
  1. Tally first-choice votes (or current holder after transfers). Elect any candidate reaching quota.
  2. Transfer all surpluses from newly elected candidates (Cincinnati Method — see 1c).
  3. After first-count surpluses are resolved: eliminate ALL candidates with fewer than 50 votes
     simultaneously; transfer their ballots to each voter's next valid continuing preference.
  4. Eliminate the single lowest-polling candidate; transfer ballots.
  5. Repeat from step 1 until all seats are filled.

Termination: once enough candidates have reached quota to fill all seats, any in-progress transfer is
completed, then all remaining continuing candidates are declared defeated and the election ends.

### 1c. Surplus Transfer — Cincinnati Method

Used when a candidate's vote total exceeds the quota.

  surplus = candidate_total - quota
  n = floor(candidate_total / surplus)

  - Number the candidate's ballots sequentially in the order they were counted
    (precinct draw order determines sequence)
  - Extract every nth ballot: #n, #2n, #3n, ...
  - Transfer each extracted ballot to the voter's next valid continuing preference
  - Exhausted ballots (no valid continuing preference) remain with the original candidate

If the n, 2n, 3n sequence doesn't yield enough ballots, continue as n+1, 2n+1, 3n+1, ...

Note: This is whole-ballot transfer (not fractional). Cambridge has used this method since 1941.

### 1d. Tie-Breaking (in priority order)

When two or more candidates are tied for last place:

  1. Compare their totals immediately before the most recent transfer. Eliminate the one who was lower
     at that prior stage. If still tied, recurse backward through all previous counts.
  2. Election official decides.
  3. Random/computer selection.

Any other tie not covered above: decided by lot.

### 1e. Cambridge-Specific Variations from Standard STV

- Cincinnati Method (whole-ballot sampling) rather than fractional transfer
- "Under 50 votes" simultaneous elimination round before single-candidate elimination begins
- Backward-recursive historical tie-breaking

## 2. Vacancy Filling — Countback Method

When a sitting councillor resigns, dies, or otherwise vacates their seat mid-term, Cambridge uses a
"countback" rather than a special election or council appointment.

Legal authority: Cambridge City Charter §7-15; MGL Chapter 54A §13
Precedent: September 19, 2024 recount following death of Councillor Joan Pickett (2,248 ballots
credited to her in the 2023 election were used)

### 2a. Countback Procedure

  1. The Election Commission takes all ballots credited to the departing councillor at the end of the
     original STV count.
  2. Ballots are re-sorted: skip any choices for candidates already elected or otherwise ineligible;
     transfer each ballot to the voter's earliest preference among unelected candidates from the
     original election.
  3. If any candidate receives more than half of the ballots showing a valid preference → elected.
  4. If not, eliminate the lowest candidate and redistribute their ballots. Repeat until one candidate
     holds more ballots than all others combined.
  5. If the winning candidate is willing and eligible, the City Clerk swears them in within 15 days.
  6. If the winner declines, the countback continues to the next candidate.

### 2b. Fallback

If no unelected candidate from the original election remains who is willing and eligible to serve, the
remaining council members fill the vacancy by majority vote for the unexpired term.

## 3. Ballot File Format (.PRM Files)

Cambridge's ballot data is stored in .PRM files produced by ChoicePlus Pro. No official public
documentation of this format has been found. The following is inferred from the files themselves and
MGL Ch. 54A.

Example files: elections/official/2025/Council_Final/*.PRM (59 files, one per precinct)
Parsing code: scripts/citylib/elections.py — loadFinalBallots()

### 3a. File Layout

Each line is one ballot:

  <ballot_id>,<batch>,<contest>,<unknown>) <rankings>

Fields:
  ballot_id — unique ballot serial number (e.g. 000101-00-00010000006231)
              The first 6 digits encode ward+precinct (e.g. 001102 = Ward 1 Precinct 1 ... or
              the filename encodes the precinct: 1102.PRM = Ward 11 Precinct 2).
              IMPORTANT: the precinct is NOT stored in any field — it comes from the filename.
  batch     — scan batch identifier (e.g. 00108); multiple batches per precinct are normal
  contest   — contest number: 001 = City Council, 002 = School Committee
  unknown   — always 1 in every observed PRM file; NOT a validity flag; purpose unknown

Rankings (after the closing paren+space):
  Comma-separated entries of the form CXX[N] where:
    CXX = candidate code (e.g. C15, C19, WI01 for write-ins)
    [N]  = rank number as marked on the physical ballot

### 3b. Ballot Validity

Validity is determined by ChoicePlus Pro during counting, NOT stored in the PRM file itself
(the field before `)` is always 1). Invalid ballots are flagged in the output reports:
  Council Final Piles Report.txt  — shows `ballot_id, 0)` for invalid
  Council Ballot Allocation Report.txt — shows `ballot_id: INVALID`

A ballot is INVALID if:
  1. No rankings at all — line ends with `1) ` (trailing space, nothing after)
     These are blank ballots (voter marked nothing) or machine-unreadable ballots.
  2. No unambiguous first choice — the voter's lowest-numbered rank has two or more
     candidates tied (= sign), and every subsequent rank also has ties (or there are none).
     Per MGL Ch. 54A: a ballot is invalid if it "cannot be taken to indicate clearly any
     first choice."

     Examples of INVALID rankings:
       C14=C15=C17=C02=C09=C11[1]               — all tied at rank 1, no clear first choice
       C19=C01                                   — two candidates tied at rank 1 (implied)
       C17=C19=C01=C02[1],C18=C03=C05[2]        — every tier has ties, no unambiguous choice

     A ballot is VALID even if some later ranks have ties, as long as the first preference
     is unambiguous (e.g. C01[1],C08=C18[13],C06[15] is valid — C01 is a clear first choice).

Invalid ballots do not participate in counting. The Election object subtracts invalid count:
  stats['total'] = stats['total'] - stats['invalid']

### 3c. Special Ranking Patterns

TIED RANKS (= sign between candidates before the bracket):
  C08=C18[13]          — voter marked both C08 and C18 at rank 13
  C14=C15=C16[1]       — voter marked all three at rank 1

  If a mid-ballot rank has ties, those candidates are skipped for that position; the ballot
  is still valid and counting continues at the next unambiguous rank.
  In loadFinalBallots(), the regex ^([CWI0-9]+)\[(\d+)\]$ does not match entries containing =,
  so they are silently skipped — correct behavior.

DUPLICATE RANKS (same [N] used for two separate, non-= entries):
  Handled in loadFinalBallots() by setting ranks[rank] = "" when a rank is seen a second time.
  Both candidates are excluded from that position; the ballot continues with the next valid rank.

SKIPPED RANKS:
  Per MGL Ch. 54A: broken numerical sequences are permitted. The statute says the smallest
  marked number = first choice, next smallest = second choice, etc., regardless of gaps.
  Example: ranks 1, 3, 5 on a ballot → treated as first, second, third choices.

EXHAUSTED BALLOTS:
  A ballot with no valid continuing preference at some point in counting is "exhausted." It
  stays with its current holder (surplus transfers) or is set aside. A ballot where all
  rankings are tied produces an empty candidate list and is exhausted from round 1 — but note
  that ChoicePlus Pro may flag such a ballot INVALID before it even enters counting (see 3b).

### 3d. Candidate Codes

Candidate codes (C01, C02, ..., WI01, etc.) are defined in a separate configuration file parsed by
load_election_configuration() and loadBallotPiles(). The .CANDIDATE directive maps codes to names:
  .CANDIDATE C01, "Lastname, Firstname"
