Cambridge STV algorithm:

Quota (Droop): ⌊Total Valid Ballots / (Seats + 1)⌋ + 1

 Round structure:
 1. Count first-choice votes; elect anyone who reaches quota
 2. Transfer surpluses using the Cincinnati Method (see below)
 3. Eliminate all candidates with fewer than 50 votes simultaneously; transfer their ballots
 4. Eliminate the single lowest candidate; transfer ballots
 5. Repeat steps 1–4 until all seats are filled
 
 Cincinnati Method (surplus transfer):
 - Compute n = ⌊Candidate's total votes / Surplus⌋
 - Number the candidate's ballots sequentially; extract every nth ballot (#n, #2n, #3n…)
 - Transfer extracted ballots to each voter's next valid continuing preference
 - Exhausted ballots stay with the original candidate
 
 Tie-breaking (in priority order):
 1. Compare the tied candidates' totals at the previous transfer — eliminate whoever was lower then; recurse backward if still tied
 2. Election official decides
 3. Random selection
 
 Cambridge-specific quirks vs. standard STV:
 - Whole-ballot sampling (Cincinnati) instead of fractional transfer
 - The "under 50 votes" simultaneous elimination round
 - Backward-recursive historical tie-breaking
 
 Statutory basis: Massachusetts General Laws Chapter 54A. Counting software: ChoicePlus Pro v2.3.4 with "Cambridge Rules."

Cambridge uses a countback — a re-sort of the original election ballots — not a special election or council appointment (though the
  latter is a fallback).

  Procedure:

  1. The Election Commission takes all ballots that were credited to the departing councillor at the end of the original STV count
  2. Any choices for already-elected or ineligible candidates are skipped; ballots are re-sorted to each voter's earliest preference
  among the remaining unelected candidates from the original election
  3. If any candidate receives more than half the ballots showing a valid preference, they're elected
  4. If not, the lowest candidate is eliminated and their ballots redistributed — repeat until one candidate has more than all others
  combined
  5. If the winner is willing and eligible, the City Clerk swears them in within 15 days
  6. If they decline, the countback continues to the next candidate

  Fallback: If no unelected candidate from the original election remains who is willing and eligible, the remaining council members fill
   the vacancy by majority vote.

  Legal basis: Cambridge City Charter §7-15; MGL Chapter 54A §13. Sources: Cambridge Election Commission notice
  (https://www.cambridgema.gov/Departments/electioncommission/news/2024/09/noticeofcambridgecitycouncilvacancyrecount) and results
  (https://www.cambridgema.gov/Departments/electioncommission/news/2024/09/vacancyrecountresults).
